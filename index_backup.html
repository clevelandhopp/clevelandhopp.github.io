BIO

Cleveland Hopp (he/him) is an award-winning filmmaker and activist from South Africa whose work explores memory, land, queer identity, and intergenerational belonging in rural South Africa. His documentary Onthou vi Fredo?winner of Best Documentary and Best LGBTQ+ Representation at the FAME Inclusive Lens Awards, screened at Giornate degli Autori's Africa Calls programme, where he also served as a jury member for the UNIMED Film Award at the 82nd Venice Film Festival in Italy. Cleveland's practice bridges cinema and research, focusing on preserving and creating an archive for unsung queer heroes in rural South Africa. He was also part of the creative team behind Carissa (Jason Jacobs & Devon Delmar), a Venice Horizons Awardnominated feature, and continues to use film as a tool for healing, resistance, and renewal.
